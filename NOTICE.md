## Why dual-licensed?

The MIT License is well-suited for software but ambiguous when applied to
prose and mathematical exposition. Creative Commons Attribution 4.0
International (CC BY 4.0) is the standard license for educational content
and provides clear, internationally-recognized terms for attribution,
remixing, and reuse.

This arrangement allows:

- Other developers to freely incorporate the code helpers and verification
  routines into their own projects under MIT terms.
- Other educators and authors to freely build on the course content under
  CC BY 4.0 terms — including in their own paid courses, university
  curricula, and derivative works — provided they give attribution.
- The author to retain the moral right to be credited in all uses.

## How to attribute

For the **content**, use the citation in `CITATION.cff` or the recommended
format in `LICENSE-CONTENT.md`.
