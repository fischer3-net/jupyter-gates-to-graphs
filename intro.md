## Discrete Mathematics for AI Security

*The formal mathematics behind how AI systems reason, fail, and get exploited.*

### Author: Robert Fischer (robert@fischer3.net)

---

Welcome to **Logic Gates to Attack Graphs**, a [fischer³ Education](https://fischer3.net) course.

This course teaches rigorous discrete mathematics through the lens of AI security. Every module connects a formal mathematical structure to a concrete threat, defense, or architectural property of AI systems.

This is not a "real-world connections" footnote at the end of a chapter. The AI and security framing *is* the motivation. The mathematics is how you reason precisely about it.

---

## Who This Is For

- Security practitioners upskilling into AI systems
- ML engineers who want formal foundations
- PhD-track students preparing for graduate coursework in AI or security
- Developers building on Agent Scrutiny who want to understand the formal threat models

---

## What You Will Build

By the end of this course you will be able to:

- Model access control policies as Boolean formulas and identify logic-level vulnerabilities
- Represent neural network architectures and agentic tool call graphs as directed graphs
- Construct attack graphs and compute minimum-cost lateral movement paths
- Formalize security properties using predicate logic and quantifiers
- Analyze protocol state machines and identify violation conditions
- Enumerate adversarial example spaces using combinatorial methods
- Reason about cryptographic primitives from first principles in modular arithmetic
